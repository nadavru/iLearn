Overview


Optimization is a core subject in ML.
What do we mean by optimization?
In the context of ML, optimization is an iterative process of attempting to (typically) minimize a real valued function. Real valued functions come into play in ML as loss functions ‚Äì functions that assess the performance of whatever ML construct (classifiers, self-driving cars, etc.) we want to create.

Since L(w) is not structured as 'standard' real valued function, obtaining a closed expression for that w would be too complicated. Instead, we would have to work gradually ‚Äì starting with some random w and work iteratively towards w values for which L decreases.
